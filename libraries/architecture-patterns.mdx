Choosing the right architecture for your iOS app shapes how your code scales, how your team collaborates, and how easily you can test it. The libraries below cover the most popular patterns along with routing solutions that work across all of them.

## Pattern selection guide

<Note>
  - **MVC**: Simple apps, small teams. Apple's default pattern — familiar but can lead to Massive View Controllers.
  - **MVVM**: Most iOS apps. Works well with SwiftUI and Combine. Clear separation of view and business logic.
  - **VIPER**: Large teams with strict separation of concerns. Higher initial overhead, excellent long-term maintainability.
  - **TCA**: Functional apps that require strong testability and predictable state. Opinionated but powerful.
  - **RIBs**: Complex apps with deeply nested features. Uber's production-proven architecture.
</Note>

  <Tab title="TCA">

    ### The Composable Architecture (TCA)

    TCA, by Point-Free, models your app as a composition of reducers operating on a single state tree. Side effects are explicit and managed through `Effect`. The pattern makes testing straightforward because you drive reducers directly in tests without any UI.

    <CardGroup cols={2}>
      <Card
        title="The Composable Architecture"
        icon="cubes-stacked"
        href="https://github.com/pointfreeco/swift-composable-architecture"
      >
        A library for building applications in a consistent, understandable way with composition, testing, and ergonomics in mind.
      </Card>
      <Card
        title="Composable Navigator"
        icon="route"
        href="https://github.com/Bahn-X/swift-composable-navigator"
      >
        Deep-linkable SwiftUI navigation built with TCA composition in mind.
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>

    <Tip>
      TCA works best when you want a single source of truth across your entire app. The learning curve is steeper than MVVM, but the debugging and testing story is exceptional.
    </Tip>

  <Tab title="RIBs / Other patterns">

    ### RIBs and other patterns

    RIBs (Router, Interactor, Builder, Presenter, View) is Uber's open-source architecture for large-scale mobile apps. Each RIB is an independently testable unit with its own lifecycle.
