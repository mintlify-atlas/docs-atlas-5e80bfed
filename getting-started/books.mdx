---
title: "Books"
description: "Recommended books for learning iOS development, Swift, and Objective-C — from beginner guides to advanced language deep-dives."
---

The books below are drawn from the [Awesome iOS](https://github.com/vsouza/awesome-ios) project's most recommended reading list. They span the full range of iOS and Apple platform development, from your very first app to expert-level Swift and Cocoa patterns.

## Beginner

Books that assume little or no prior iOS or Swift experience.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card
    title="Anyone Can Create an App"
    icon="mobile"
    href="https://www.manning.com/books/anyone-can-create-an-app"
  >
    By Wendy L. Wise. A hands-on guide for absolute beginners who want to build their first iOS app without a programming background.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="Hello Swift!"
    icon="hand-wave"
    href="https://www.manning.com/books/hello-swift"
  >
    By Tanmay Bakshi with Lynn Beighley. A beginner-friendly introduction to Swift and iOS app development, written for young developers and newcomers alike.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="iOS Development with Swift"
    icon="apple"
    href="https://www.manning.com/books/ios-development-with-swift"
  >
    By Craig Grummitt. Covers the full iOS development workflow using Swift, from setting up Xcode to submitting to the App Store.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="The Swift Programming Language"
    icon="swift"
    href="https://books.apple.com/us/book/swift-programming-language/id881256329"
  >
    By Apple. The official reference for the Swift language. Available free from Apple Books — the canonical starting point for any Swift developer.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Intermediate

Books for developers comfortable with Swift basics who want to build deeper knowledge.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card
    title="iOS Programming: The Big Nerd Ranch Guide"
    icon="ranch"
    href="https://www.bignerdranch.com/books/ios-programming-the-big-nerd-ranch-guide-seventh-edition/"
  >
    By Christian Keur and Aaron Hillegass. The classic Big Nerd Ranch iOS guide — comprehensive coverage of UIKit, data persistence, networking, and more.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="Classic Computer Science Problems in Swift"
    icon="binary"
    href="https://www.manning.com/books/classic-computer-science-problems-in-swift"
  >
    Tackles fundamental CS problems — search algorithms, dynamic programming, graph problems — implemented in Swift.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="Programming in Objective-C"
    icon="c"
    href="https://www.amazon.com/Programming-Objective-C-6th-Developers-Library/dp/0321967607"
  >
    By Stephen G. Kochan. The definitive guide to Objective-C, essential reading for maintaining or understanding legacy iOS codebases.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="Cocoa Design Patterns"
    icon="shapes"
    href="https://www.amazon.com/Cocoa-Design-Patterns-Erik-Buck/dp/0321535022"
  >
    By Erik Buck and Donald Yacktman. A thorough examination of the design patterns underpinning Cocoa and iOS SDK frameworks.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="Core Data"
    icon="database"
    href="https://www.objc.io/books/core-data/"
  >
    By Florian Kugler and Daniel Eggert. A practical and deeply detailed guide to Core Data, Apple's persistence framework, from the objc.io team.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="Swift in Depth"
    icon="arrow-down"
    href="https://www.manning.com/books/swift-in-depth"
  >
    Explores intermediate to advanced Swift patterns — protocols, generics, enums, and more — for developers who already know the basics.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Advanced

Books for experienced iOS developers looking to master Swift and underlying system patterns.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card
    title="Advanced Swift"
    icon="bolt"
    href="https://www.objc.io/books/advanced-swift/"
  >
    By Chris Eidhof, Ole Begemann, and Airspeed Velocity. A deep exploration of the Swift language: generics, protocols, performance, and the standard library internals.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="Functional Swift"
    icon="function"
    href="https://www.objc.io/books/functional-swift/"
  >
    By Chris Eidhof, Florian Kugler, and Wouter Swierstra. Teaches functional programming concepts — map, filter, reduce, and more — as applied to Swift.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="The Complete Friday Q&A, Volume 1"
    icon="circle-question"
    href="https://www.mikeash.com/book.html"
  >
    By Mike Ash. A compilation of the celebrated Friday Q&A blog series, covering low-level Objective-C runtime, memory management, and system internals.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  Several books from objc.io (Advanced Swift, Functional Swift, Core Data) are updated regularly to reflect new Swift and platform releases. Check the publisher's site for the latest edition.
</Note>
